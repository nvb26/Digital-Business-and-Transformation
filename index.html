Why this segment?

Young urban consumers (18–35) represent the ideal first-mover market for EcoScan & Plan. They are digital natives who already use their smartphones in-store, they have strong sustainability values, and yet they consistently report that actually shopping sustainably is too time-consuming and confusing. This gap between intention and action is exactly what our app bridges.

🎯 Market size & behavior

4.2 million Dutch consumers aged 18–35 do regular grocery shopping. 73% indicate they want sustainable options (Eurobarometer 2024), but only 26% actively choose them — a massive intention-action gap. The Dutch grocery market is worth €48B annually, with sustainability-labeled products growing 12% year-over-year.

📈 Proven demand signals

Yuka (product scanning app) reached 2M Dutch users in 18 months — proving that consumers want in-store scanning tools. However, Yuka focuses on health, not sustainability. Our app fills this specific gap in the retail consumer journey.

⚡ Why digital transformation fits

Traditional retail relies on physical labels and static information. Our solution digitally transforms the in-store experience by layering real-time AI-powered insights directly onto the consumer's shopping journey — bridging the offline retail world with digital intelligence.

Digital Transformation in Retail

How we digitally transform grocery shopping

Traditional grocery retail is built on physical shelves, static labels, and uninformed consumers. EcoScan & Plan introduces a digital layer that fundamentally changes how customers interact with products in-store.

🏪

From Static Labels to Real-Time Intelligence

Retail currently relies on physical eco-labels that are fragmented and hard to compare. We digitize this by aggregating data from multiple databases (Open Food Facts, EU Ecolabel, supply chain data) and delivering a unified score instantly via mobile — transforming the point-of-purchase moment from guessing into informed decision-making.

🤖

AI as the New In-Store Advisor

Where traditional retail depends on shelf placement and promotions to guide choices, our AI acts as a personalized sustainability advisor. It analyzes product data, learns from user preferences, and suggests better alternatives — creating a new digital touchpoint in the physical retail journey (Verhoef et al., 2021).

📊

Data-Driven Value for the Retail Ecosystem

Beyond the consumer, we generate aggregated sustainability insights for supermarkets and brands — enabling retailers to optimize sustainable assortments and brands to understand green consumer behavior. This creates a two-sided data network effect that strengthens over time (Hagiu & Wright, 2020).

Feasibility

How we make this work in practice

A realistic roadmap from data to product — showing investors this isn't just an idea, but an executable plan.

🗃

Data: Where do we get it?

Our sustainability scores are built on existing, proven open-data infrastructure — not built from scratch.

  • ✓ Open Food Facts — open-source database with 3M+ products, including Dutch supermarkets (AH, Jumbo, Lidl)
  • ✓ EU PEF (Product Environmental Footprint) — standardized environmental impact data
  • ✓ Eco-label APIs — integration with existing certification databases (FSC, Rainforest Alliance, EU Ecolabel)
  • ✓ Barcode lookup — EAN/UPC global barcode registry for product identification
🤖

AI: How reliable is it?

We use AI as an aggregator and recommender — not as the sole source of truth. This keeps accuracy high and hallucination risk low.

  • ✓ Scoring model — rule-based scoring (CO₂, packaging, sourcing) with AI for edge cases only
  • ✓ Meal planning — GPT-based recipe generation with constraint filters (seasonal, local, dietary)
  • ✓ Human-in-the-loop — flagged products reviewed by sustainability experts before database entry
  • ✓ Transparency — users see the breakdown behind every score, not just a single number
🚀

MVP: How do we start?

We launch lean and expand based on traction. The MVP focuses on the core scan feature in one market.

  • ✓ Phase 1 (0-6 months) — Barcode scanner + sustainability score for top 50K Dutch products. Launch in Groningen as test market.
  • ✓ Phase 2 (6-12 months) — Add AI meal planner and smart shopping list. Expand to Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Utrecht.
  • ✓ Phase 3 (12-18 months) — B2B dashboard for retailers. First supermarket integration pilot.
  • ✓ Tech stack — React Native (cross-platform), Python/FastAPI backend, PostgreSQL, hosted on AWS.

Ethics, Sustainability & AI Risks

Due diligence for responsible innovation

We take the ethical, legal, and sustainability risks of our product seriously — and have concrete mitigation strategies for each.

⚠ Identified Risks

Data privacy & GDPR

The app collects shopping behavior, dietary preferences, and location data — all personal data under GDPR. Risk of non-compliance or data breaches.

AI bias & score reliability

Sustainability scores depend on data quality. Incomplete databases or biased training data could unfairly disadvantage certain brands or product categories.

Greenwashing facilitation

If brands pay for visibility in recommendations, there's a risk of blurring the line between genuine sustainability and paid promotion.

Digital exclusion

App-only access excludes older consumers or those without smartphones, potentially widening the sustainability information gap.

Environmental cost of AI

Running AI models requires significant computing power and energy. The app's own carbon footprint could undermine its sustainability message.

✅ Mitigation Strategies

Privacy by design

GDPR-compliant from day one. Data minimization, on-device processing where possible, transparent data usage policy, and opt-in only for personalization features. Annual third-party privacy audit.

Transparent scoring methodology

Open-source our scoring algorithm. Show users the full breakdown behind every score. Use human expert review for contested products. Regular methodology audits with independent sustainability researchers.

Editorial independence policy

Strict separation between paid placements and organic recommendations. Paid content always labeled. Independent editorial board reviews the recommendation algorithm quarterly.

Accessibility roadmap

Phase 2 includes a web-app version. Explore partnerships with supermarkets for in-store screens. Design for WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility standards.

Green AI commitment

Use energy-efficient models (smallest model fit for purpose). Host on green cloud providers (AWS EU regions with renewable energy). Track and offset our own computational carbon footprint.
